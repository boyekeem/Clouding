Champions League

Player Ratings: Real Madrid 2-0 Roma (agg 4-0)

Next Previous

By Ben Hayward

0
8 Mar 2016 22:45:00

Los Blancos beat Roma 2-0 to advance to the quarter-finals, but they will need to find some dramatic improvement to have any hope of winning the trophy at San Siro in May

COMMENT  
Real Madrid live to fight another day in the Champions League. Zinedine Zidane's side weathered the storm and ultimately beat Roma 2-0 on Tuesday to advance to the last eight of the continental competition. But it was far from convincing and playing like that, they have no chance of lifting the trophy in May.

With a 2-0 lead from the first leg, this second match should have been straightforward in what was the Frenchman's Champions League bow at the Bernabeu. In his pre-match press conference on Monday, Zidane had said he wanted Real to win and win well. It sounded good.

But the 43-year-old sent out the wrong message with his team selection. First of all, he left promising youngster Borja Mayoral out of the squad altogether despite the absence of Karim Benzema. And on Tuesday, Lucas Vazquez was only on the bench when it looked like the winger's energy was just what Real required. James Rodriguez, in poor form and out of shape, was in the starting XI though, as was the dreadful Danilo in place of Dani Carvajal.

All of that handed the initiative to Roma. The Italian side pressed high up the pitch, pegged Madrid pack and created several clear chances in the opening hour. Mohamed Salah squandered two glorious opportunities, Edin Dzeko another and it looked like it would not be their night. It wasn't.


Had Roma gone ahead as they should have, Madrid may have found life tough, but instead Cristiano Ronaldo gave the home side the lead with a goal created by Lucas, who had been on the pitch for just a few moments before beating his marker and finding the Portuguese with a lovely cross. And minutes later, James made it two. The scoreline did not tell the whole story, though.

Yes, Madrid created more chances, but Roma had carved out the best opportunities and if the final result was fair, it was only because the Italian side were unable to convert while Real netted two of theirs.

Madrid march on then to the last eight of the competition, but on such form they seem unlikely to trouble any of the top teams like Barcelona or Bayern Munich in the quarter-finals or later on. Conceding chances as they did today, Real would be blown away by such sides - and they will need to improve dramatically in the latter rounds.

"That performance won't scare anyone in the Champions League," former England midfielder Owen Hargreaves told BT Sport. "Before you would have thought Real Madrid are one of the top teams but that was careless. They could have been down in that game but the only difference was they took their chances. Roma were so poor in front of goal, they should have scored at least one or maybe two goals."

Madrid are already off the pace in La Liga and were thrown out of the Copa del Rey earlier this season for fielding the suspended Denis Cheryshev by mistake. The Champions League, therefore, is the only objective left this term for the Spanish side.

"Before we can think about that, we have a number of games ahead," Zidane said. "We have to think about our next game and then the one after that." And he added: "Today, I am happy with the win. I thought we attacked well and defended well."

However, Madrid lacked intensity and were pulled apart by Roma on a number of occasions, with goalkeeper Keylor Navas to thank for keeping them in it, or at least the scores level until Ronaldo fired the home team in front on the hour.

For his part, Roma boss Luciano Spalletti was furious. "We lacked quality in our finishing," he said. "We should have taken our chances. We are mentally weak."







It was a refreshingly honest assessment of his team's travails on Tuesday night and one which will win him the respect of Roma's fans. Because the match was there for the taking and his players were unable to put Madrid to the sword.

Zidane's discourse is different. The Frenchman was all smiles and probably relieved to have come through his first tough test in the Champions League, perhaps more so to have achieved a result that keeps president Florentino Perez content on the night of the club chief's 69th birthday.

But behind the scenes, Zidane would do well to take a leaf out of Spalletti's book and pick apart a performance that was nowhere near satisfactory. On this evidence, he has to give the harder-working younger players a chance and also find a solution to the team's profligacy ahead of the quarter-final matches.

Because if Madrid play again as they did on Tuesday, they can forget all about claiming Champions League glory in Italy at the end of May.
